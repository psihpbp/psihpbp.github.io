Meet the Consultants


John Scott is a senior at the University of Chicago majoring in Economics. John is the project leader for the Hyde Park Business Partnership and Social Media Chair for Phoenix Sustainability Initiative. He is interested in environmental economics, sustainable development, and global hunger, and hopes to leverage his knowledge and skills to improve sustainable business practices in Hyde Park.

John Scott

 
Deima Tankus is a fourth-year Economics major in the college, and holds a deep interest in environmentally and economically sustainable business strategy and urban development. Born and raised in forest-filled Lithuania, she has a strong appreciation for the interactions between natural systems and the built environment.

Deimante Tankeviciute

 
Linda Trey is a third year Environmental Science major in the college, and has been involved with PSI for two years. Her past experience with sustainability has involved working for organizations such as the Nature Conservancy and Clean Water Action. Being from New York, she sees cities as full of potential in helping to achieve a greener future. She is especially interested in reducing food and plastic waste, and believes that consumer choice and sustainable business practices will play a large role.

Linda Trey

 
Danielle Lee is a second-year student at the University of Chicago studying neuroscience. In previous years, she has performed ecology research on the Chesapeake Bay, during which she became interested in environmental and sustainability issues. She is passionate about implementing energy-efficient practices and about educating others about the role they can play in reducing their carbon footprints. She is excited to be a new member of the Phoenix Sustainability Initiative team this year.

Danielle Lee

 
Jiaqi Gao is a first-year and prospective computer science major in the college. Having lived in rural farmlands and megalopolises, she sees the significance of balancing both commercial growth and sustainability. While her focus is in the field of technology, she is motivated by her passion for environmental science and hopes to use her knowledge to help implement sustainable practices in Hyde Park businesses.

Jiaqi Gao

 
Kriza Sy is a third-year Biology major in the College. She is especially interested in sharing accessible ways consumers can practice sustainable lifestyles and redirecting and repurposing various forms of waste. Last year, Kriza led a project in making student-run cafes on the University campus more sustainable and she looks forward to branching out with this experience into the broader Hyde Park business community.

Kriza Sy

Claudia Yanos is a first-year in the college and a prospective Computational and Applied Mathematics and Geophysical Sciences Major. Having grown up on a New York City block with five different community gardens, she is well-steeped in knowledge about sustainable practices for the urban environment. Especially after working at NASA’s Climate Change Research Initiative, she is enthused about Environmental Science, and committed to helping businesses reach their sustainable practice implementation goals.

Claudia Yanos

 
Max Lopez is a third-year Environmental Studies and Global Studies double major. He is interested in the intersection between business and environmental policy and will be pursuing further studies in environmental law after graduation.

Max Lopez
